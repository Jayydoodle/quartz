A cryptographic key that allows a user to receive cryptocurrency from another user, but cannot be used to send funds. They're unique and usually consist of 64 characters to encrypt your wallet or make digital signatures.  Private keys represent the final control and ownership of cryptocurrency. If your keys are stolen or lost, your crypto is gone.  [[Not Your Keys, Not Your Coins]]

Unlike your [[Public Key]], you should <font color="#ff0000">never give your private key out to anyone</font>
